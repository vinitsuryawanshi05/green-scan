Plant Information

Ashoka Tree (Saraca asoca)

Scientific Name: Saraca asoca

Description: The Ashoka tree is a large evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent. Known for its bright yellow-orange flowers, it is considered a symbol of love and fertility in Indian culture. The tree is often planted in gardens and temples due to its beautiful, fragrant blooms.

Care Tips:

  • Water: Requires moderate watering, but prefers a well-drained soil environment.
  • Light: Thrives in full sunlight or partial shade.
  • Temperature: Best grown in tropical climates with temperatures between 20°C and 35°C.
  • Soil: Prefers well-drained, loamy soil with a slightly acidic pH.

Uses:

The Ashoka tree is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional Ayurvedic medicine. It is often used to treat various ailments like menstrual disorders and digestive issues.
