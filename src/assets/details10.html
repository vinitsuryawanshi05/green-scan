Plant Information

Gamhar Tree (Gmelina arborea)

Scientific Name: Gmelina arborea

Description: The Gamhar Tree, also known as Gmelina or Gumhar, is a fast-growing deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent and Southeast Asia. It is commonly used in agroforestry and timber production due to its straight trunk and quality wood. The tree has large, heart-shaped leaves and bears trumpet-shaped yellow flowers with purple markings. It is valued not only for its wood but also for its ecological and medicinal benefits.

Care Tips:

  • Water: Requires moderate watering, especially in the early stages. Drought-tolerant once established.
  • Light: Prefers full sun but can tolerate light shade.
  • Temperature: Thrives in warm tropical climates, ideally between 25°C to 35°C.
  • Soil: Grows well in well-drained loamy or sandy soils. It can tolerate poor and degraded soils too.

Medicinal Uses:

The Gamhar Tree has several medicinal applications, especially in traditional Ayurvedic medicine. The bark and roots are used for treating digestive issues, fever, and inflammation. The leaves have antiseptic properties and are used in poultices for wounds and skin conditions. The fruit is used as a mild laxative and also in the treatment of anemia and liver disorders. Its multipurpose nature makes it valuable both medicinally and commercially.
